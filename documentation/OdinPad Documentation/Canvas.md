# **Canvas: OdinPad's Story Planning Studio**

## **1. Vision & Purpose**

**Canvas** is OdinPad's story architecture studio—the space where the raw materials forged in the **Idea Web** and shaped in the **Sandbox** become a complete, structured narrative. If the Idea Web is about *capturing* creative sparks and the Sandbox is about *expanding* them, Canvas is about *assembling* them into a coherent whole.

Canvas provides a suite of visual and structural tools designed to help writers see their story from every angle. Whether you're a plotter building intricate architectures or a pantser discovering structure as you go, Canvas adapts to your process—offering just enough structure to keep you oriented without constraining your creativity.

Canvas sits directly after the Sandbox in the natural creative flow, serving as the essential middle layer that transforms the creative output of the Sandbox (expanded ideas, conversations, lists, mind maps, deep dives) into a clear, rearrangeable, and visually intuitive story blueprint before any prose is committed in the Manuscript.

**Core Philosophy:** Every story has a structure. Canvas helps you find yours.

## **3. Core Philosophy: Structure That Reveals**

Canvas operates on the principle that structure should *reveal* the story, not constrain it. Different writers need different levels of structure at different times:

| **Planning Style** | **How Canvas Supports**                                         |
| ------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Architect**      | Detailed hierarchical binder, beat templates, custom metadata   |
| **Gardener**       | Flexible corkboard, draggable scenes, organic arrangement       |
| **Weaver**         | Multi-thread timeline, parallel plotlines, relationship mapping |
| **Explorer**       | Visual canvas, freeform placement, discover-as-you-go           |
| **Remodeler**      | Multiple views of same structure, easy reorganization           |

Canvas offers multiple entry points: start with a template, build from scratch with cards, or import developed elements from Sandbox and arrange them visually. All paths lead to the same integrated experience.

Canvas feels like a giant, infinite corkboard in a warm writer's den—soft lamplight glow, parchment textures, gentle drag physics—where writers can pin, shuffle, connect, and layer story pieces until the narrative rhythm feels right. It supports rigid plotters (templates, timelines, beat sheets) and free-spirited pantsers (loose card clouds, spatial clustering) equally well.

**Core Philosophy Fit:**

- Makes structure playful and low-pressure—no forced commitment to prose yet
- Prevents pacing disasters, timeline contradictions, and emotional flat spots before drafting begins
- Creates a living bridge: Sandbox explosions become movable Canvas pieces; Canvas decisions feed directly into Manuscript scenes
- AI is always assistant: detects gaps, suggests connections/placements, flags inconsistencies, auto-links entities—never writes beats or synopses
- Ties deeply into Gamification system for habit rewards (e.g., "Pacing Master" badge for reordering 15+ scenes)

## **4. The Canvas Components**

Canvas consists of six interconnected tools, each offering a different perspective on your story's structure:

1. **The Binder** — Hierarchical organization and navigation
2. **The Corkboard** — Visual index card layout for scenes
3. **The Timeline** — Chronological plotting with parallel threads
4. **The Blueprint** — Story structure templates and beat mapping
5. **The Atlas** — Relationship mapping and visual planning
6. **The Observatory** — Multiple synchronized views of your structure

## **5. The Canvas Workflow: From Sandbox to Manuscript**

Canvas is designed as the natural "next step" after Sandbox sessions. The flow is deliberate and frictionless, representing a flexible, non-linear workflow that adapts to how writers actually work. The journey from Sandbox to Manuscript is not a rigid pipeline but a dynamic process where writers move between tools as their story develops.

### **5.1 Workflow Integration: The Sandbox-to-Canvas Bridge**

Every successful Sandbox session ends with a prominent **"Send to Canvas"** button. Sandbox outputs appear as ready-to-place cards or nodes in Canvas:

| **Sandbox Output**          | **Becomes in Canvas**                                               |
| --------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Conversation thread         | Character-focused scene cards with voice notes attached             |
| List Builder items          | Individual twist cards ready to sequence                            |
| Mind-map clusters           | Preserved sub-graphs that can be expanded or collapsed              |
| Expansion Canvas deep dives | Rich, metadata-heavy cards containing backstory, motivations, flaws |
| Braindump sections          | Collapsible note clusters attached to relevant cards                |

Changes made in Canvas optionally sync back to Sandbox for further refinement, creating a living loop: Sandbox → Canvas → Sandbox → Manuscript.

### **5.2 Entry Points: Choose Your Path**

Canvas recognizes that writers enter the structural phase at different moments and with different needs:

### **Path A: The Architect**

**Start with Structure, Then Fill**

1. **Blueprint Selection:** Choose a story structure template (Three-Act, Hero's Journey, etc.)
2. **Beat Expansion:** Expand each beat into chapter and scene concepts
3. **Timeline Creation:** Build out parallel plotlines and character arcs
4. **Corkboard Population:** Create scene cards from timeline
5. **Scene Development:** Pull elements from Sandbox to populate each scene
6. **Manuscript Generation:** Structure becomes manuscript outline

*Best for:* Plotters, genre writers, first-time novelists seeking guidance

### **Path B: The Gardener**

**Grow Structure Organically**

1. **Sandbox First:** Develop characters, settings, and plot fragments
2. **Atlas Mapping:** Visualize relationships between Sandbox elements
3. **Corkboard Assembly:** Drag Sandbox elements onto corkboard as scene seeds
4. **Organic Clustering:** Watch scenes naturally group into chapters and acts
5. **Timeline Discovery:** Arrange scenes chronologically as they emerge
6. **Blueprint Reflection:** Compare emergent structure to templates (optional)

*Best for:* Pantsers, discovery writers, literary fiction

### **Path C: The Weaver**

**Build Multiple Threads in Parallel**

1. **Timeline First:** Create plotline rows for each thread (POV characters, subplots)
2. **Thread Development:** Pull character arcs from Sandbox into timeline rows
3. **Scene Placement:** Add scene cards where threads intersect
4. **Balance Refinement:** Use AI thread balance indicators to ensure equitable distribution
5. **Corkboard Detail:** Expand timeline scenes into detailed cards
6. **Binder Organization:** Generate hierarchical structure from completed timeline

*Best for:* Ensemble casts, complex narratives, series writers

### **Path D: The Explorer**

**Discover Structure Through Play**

1. **Atlas Exploration:** Place all Sandbox elements on infinite canvas
2. **Connection Discovery:** Draw relationships, watch clusters emerge
3. **Freeform Corkboard:** Arrange cards experimentally, no forced order
4. **Pattern Recognition:** Use AI to identify emerging structural patterns
5. **Gradual Structuring:** Convert discoveries into Binder organization
6. **Timeline Confirmation:** Verify chronological flow after structure emerges

*Best for:* Experimental writers, complex worldbuilding, nonlinear narratives

### **Phase 1: Pre-Draft Structuring**

- Import developed elements from Sandbox
- Build initial structure using chosen entry path
- AI analyzes structure, flags gaps and inconsistencies
- Writer refines until structure feels solid
- Generate manuscript outline from Canvas

### **Phase 2: Drafting with Canvas Reference**

- Write in Manuscript with Canvas open in split view
- Reference structure while maintaining flexibility
- Mark scenes as "In Progress" in Canvas as you draft
- Track progress against outline

### **Phase 3: Mid-Draft Structural Adjustments**

- New scenes emerge during drafting—add them to Canvas
- Characters develop unexpectedly—update Atlas relationships
- Pacing feels off—use AI analysis to identify issues
- Restructure in Canvas; manuscript outline updates automatically

### **Phase 4: Post-Draft Structural Revision**

- Complete first draft, import into Canvas
- AI analyzes full structure against intentions
- Identify pacing issues, thread imbalances, arc problems
- Use Restructure Mode to test alternate organizations
- Apply changes; manuscript restructures automatically
- Iterate until structure matches vision

### **5.4 Promotion to Manuscript**

When ready, select one or more cards → **"Send to Manuscript"** → creates corresponding scene documents in Binder/Manuscript with:

- Title and synopsis from card
- Linked Story Wiki entities
- Metadata (POV, tone, tags) pre-filled
- Placeholder text block ready for prose

Original Canvas card remains linked for reference; changes in Manuscript (e.g., word count) update card progress bar.

### **5.5 Bidirectional Sync & Iteration Loop**

- Manuscript changes (new scene added, reordered) → auto-reflect in Canvas (new card appears, order updates)
- Canvas rearrangements → update Binder and Manuscript sequence
- Story Wiki updates (character motivation change) → prompt Canvas user: "Update affected scene cards?"
- Sandbox refinements → "Re-import to Canvas" button pulls updated versions back into the workspace

This loop makes Canvas a living, breathing planning surface—always in sync with the rest of the project.

## **6. AI Features in Canvas**

Aligned with OdinPad's **AI Philosophy (0% generative text)** , Canvas AI acts as a **"Structural Architect's Assistant"** —it analyzes, suggests, identifies, and reveals, but never writes prose or imposes structure without user approval. All AI is non-generative, local/edge-capable where possible, user-approval required for any action, and focused on detection, suggestion, visualization, and consistency.

### **6.1 Structural Intelligence**

### **Pacing Analysis & Visualization**

- **Scene Density Mapping:** AI analyzes scene length and complexity across your manuscript, generating a heat map showing where action clusters, where scenes drag, and where pacing might need adjustment.
- **Tension Arc Detection:** Automatically maps emotional intensity across your narrative, identifying peaks and valleys. Suggests where tension might need to rise or fall based on genre conventions and your selected blueprint.
- **Scene Length Optimization:** Analyzes scene lengths against genre norms and your writing patterns. Flags unusually long or short scenes that might disrupt flow.
- **Rest Spot Identification:** Identifies where readers might need breathing room between intense sequences. Suggests potential placement for quieter, reflective scenes.

### **Structural Gap Analysis**

- **Missing Beat Detection:** Compares your current structure against your selected blueprint (or common structural patterns) and identifies missing beats. Example: *"Your story has an Inciting Incident and a Midpoint, but no visible First Plot Point. Consider adding a scene where the protagonist commits to the journey."*
- **Plot Hole Identification:** AI analyzes cause-and-effect chains across your timeline, flagging logical inconsistencies. Example: *"Character A learns the secret in Chapter 4 but acts as if unaware until Chapter 12. Is there a missing scene where they decide to hide their knowledge?"*
- **Thread Drop Detection:** Identifies plot threads, character arcs, or subplots that appear and then vanish without resolution. Example: *"The mystery of the missing artifact was introduced in Chapter 3 and hasn't appeared since. Consider a reminder beat in Act 2."*
- **Setup-Payoff Analysis:** Scans for introduced elements (objects, character traits, promises) that lack payoff, and payoffs without proper setup.

### **Character Arc Intelligence**

- **Arc Progression Tracking:** Maps each character's emotional journey across your structure, identifying where key arc moments should occur and flagging when a character hasn't developed sufficiently.
- **Appearance Frequency Analysis:** Tracks how often each character appears, identifying characters who have disappeared for too long or appear too infrequently to maintain reader connection.
- **Relationship Evolution Mapping:** Analyzes how relationships between characters change over time, flagging when a relationship shift occurs without proper development.
- **Voice Consistency Indicators:** Compares dialogue and internal monologue patterns across scenes, highlighting potential voice inconsistencies.

### **6.2 Timeline Intelligence**

### **Parallel Plotline Management**

- **Thread Balance Indicators:** AI analyzes the distribution of plot threads across your timeline, providing a visual balance score. Alerts when one thread dominates or when a thread has been absent too long.
- **Convergence Optimization:** Identifies opportunities where plotlines could intersect for maximum dramatic effect. Suggests potential convergence points based on thematic resonance.
- **Timeline Conflict Detection:** Flags chronological inconsistencies. Example: *"Scene 14 takes place on Day 3, but Scene 15 (Day 2) appears later. Chronological order may need adjustment."*
- **Pacing Synchronization:** Ensures parallel plotlines maintain complementary pacing—if one thread is high-intensity, another might need to breathe, and vice versa.
- **Travel Time & Continuity Guardian:** Auto-detects date/time mentions in card synopses or linked Story Wiki events. Real-time alerts for impossible travel, age jumps, pregnancy duration errors, moon-phase mismatches. Suggests "travel montage" or "time-skip" cards when gaps are detected.

### **Scene Sequencing Intelligence**

- **Optimal Scene Order Suggestions:** AI analyzes your scenes and suggests alternative sequences that might improve dramatic tension, cause-and-effect logic, or emotional impact. User reviews and approves/disapproves each suggestion.
- **Flashback Placement Analysis:** Evaluates whether flashbacks appear at optimal moments for emotional impact and narrative clarity. Suggests repositioning when flashbacks disrupt momentum or appear without sufficient context.
- **Foreshadowing Optimization:** Identifies where early scenes could better set up later revelations. Suggests opportunities to plant seeds for future twists.

### **6.3 Corkboard Intelligence**

### **Visual Pattern Recognition**

- **Emotional Arc Visualization:** AI analyzes scene metadata (emotional tone, stakes, conflict intensity) and generates a color-coded overlay across your corkboard, showing the emotional journey at a glance. Cards colored by intensity (cool blue → hot red).
- **Pacing Heat Map:** Overlays a heat map showing where tension peaks and valleys occur. Red zones indicate high intensity; blue zones indicate lower energy.
- **Cluster Detection:** Automatically identifies groups of scenes that share themes, settings, characters, or emotional tones. Suggests whether these clusters should be consolidated, spread out, or given more connective tissue.
- **Scene Type Distribution:** Analyzes the balance of scene types (action, dialogue, introspection, exposition) across your manuscript. Flags when one type dominates excessively.

### **Scene Relationship Intelligence**

- **Sequel-Connection Detection:** Identifies scenes that logically follow others and suggests when they might be too far apart. Example: *"Scene 8 (the argument) is followed by Scene 15 (the reconciliation). Consider bridging scenes to show emotional progression."*
- **Scene Redundancy Detection:** Flags scenes that serve similar purposes or hit similar emotional beats too close together. Suggests combining or spacing them out.
- **Scene Necessity Analysis:** Evaluates whether each scene pulls its weight—advances plot, develops character, or reveals necessary information. Flags scenes that might be cut or combined.

### **6.4 Atlas Intelligence**

### **Relationship Mapping**

- **Network Analysis:** AI analyzes your character and faction relationships, identifying:
  - **Central Nodes:** Most-connected characters (protagonists, key influencers)
  - **Isolated Nodes:** Characters with few connections (potential for subplots)
  - **Bridges:** Characters connecting otherwise separate groups
  - **Faction Clusters:** Natural groupings of allied characters
- **Missing Connection Suggestions:** Identifies relationships that might logically exist based on character proximity, shared history, or narrative needs. Example: *"Character B and Character C both served in the same war but have no relationship defined. Could this be a connection worth exploring?"*
- **Relationship Evolution Timeline:** Visualizes how relationships change over the course of your story—where alliances form, break, or shift.

### **Thematic & Motif Mapping**

- **Theme Detection:** AI analyzes your scenes and identifies recurring themes, symbols, and motifs. Presents these in a visual map showing where each theme appears.
- **Theme Density Analysis:** Shows which parts of your story explore each theme most heavily. Identifies underdeveloped themes you've mentioned but not fully explored.
- **Symbol Tracking:** Tracks recurring symbols across your manuscript, ensuring consistent use and meaningful payoff.
- **Thematic Highlighter:** Detects recurring tags/keywords across cards → suggests thematic threads ("Betrayal appears in 9 cards—consider strengthening as central motif?"). Visual overlay: color threads connecting related cards (e.g., red line for betrayal arc).

### **6.5 Blueprint Intelligence**

### **Structural Guidance**

- **Blueprint Adherence Score:** AI compares your structure against your selected template (or common structural patterns) and provides an adherence score. Highlights which beats are hit, which are missed, and which are hit out of order.
- **Beat Timing Analysis:** Evaluates whether story beats occur at appropriate points in your narrative. Example: *"Your Midpoint occurs at 35% of your manuscript. For this genre, Midpoint typically occurs between 45-55%. Consider whether this early Midpoint serves your story."*
- **Structural Alternatives:** Suggests alternative structural approaches that might better serve your material. Example: *"Your story has strong romantic elements. Have you considered the Romancing the Beat structure alongside your current Three-Act outline?"*

### **Custom Blueprint Intelligence**

- **Pattern Learning:** When you create custom blueprints, AI learns your structural preferences and suggests beat placements in future projects based on your patterns.
- **Genre-Specific Suggestions:** AI provides genre-appropriate beat suggestions based on your project's genre tags.

### **6.6 Observatory Intelligence**

### **Holistic Analysis**

- **Structural Health Dashboard:** A unified view of all AI analysis—pacing scores, thread balance, character arc health, structural completeness—in a single, scannable dashboard.
- **Priority Recommendations:** AI prioritizes issues based on impact, suggesting which structural problems to address first.
- **Change Impact Simulation:** Before making structural changes, AI simulates how moving, adding, or removing scenes will affect pacing, character arcs, and plot threads.

### **Revision Intelligence**

- **Before/After Comparison:** When restructuring, AI provides side-by-side comparison of old and new structures, highlighting changes and their predicted impact.
- **Risk Assessment:** Flags potential unintended consequences of structural changes. Example: *"Moving Scene 12 earlier may weaken the foreshadowing for the Chapter 7 reveal. Consider reinforcing that setup elsewhere."*

### **6.7 Entity & Consistency Checker**

- Scans all cards for Story Wiki mentions → flags missing introductions ("Elara referenced but no earlier appearance")
- Highlights contradictions ("Magic system forbids fire spells, but card 14 shows fire spell")
- Auto-suggests links: "This mentions 'the Whisperer'—link to existing Story Wiki entry?"

### **6.8 Smart Placement Suggestions**

- When dragging a card, faint "drop zones" appear: "Best fit: Act II rising action" or "Parallel to subplot B"
- Based on template, emotional curve, timeline logic, and existing card density

### **6.9 Gap & Missing Element Detector**

- "No character introduction scene for Kofi—add one in Act I?"
- "Subplot C has only one card—consider expanding?"
- "Act III lacks a climax beat—insert here?"

### **6.10 Preview Modes**

- **"Reader View":** Hides metadata, shows only synopses in sequence
- **"Emotional Heat Map":** Cards colored by intensity (cool blue → hot red)
- **"POV Distribution":** Visual breakdown of viewpoint balance

### **6.11 AI Interaction Principles**

| **Principle**              | **Implementation**                                                                                                 |
| -------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **0% Generative Text**     | AI never writes prose, dialogue, or narrative content. All suggestions are analytical, structural, or directional. |
| **User Approval Required** | Every structural suggestion requires explicit user approval. AI never automatically restructures content.          |
| **Explainability**         | Every AI insight includes a clear explanation of why the suggestion was made and what impact it might have.        |
| **Opt-In Depth**           | Users can choose their level of AI engagement—from full structural intelligence to minimal suggestions.            |
| **Learning Feedback Loop** | AI learns from user approvals and rejections, improving suggestions over time.                                     |
| **Privacy First**          | All AI analysis happens locally when possible; cloud processing is opt-in and anonymized.                          |

### **6.12 The AI-Guided Workflow**

AI in Canvas acts as a silent partner throughout the workflow:

**Step 1: Initial Assessment**

When you first import Sandbox elements into Canvas, AI provides:

- **Structural Potential Score:** How well your developed elements might form a complete structure
- **Missing Element Identification:** What key elements (character arcs, plot points) might need development
- **Blueprint Recommendations:** Which structure templates might best suit your material

**Step 2: Active Structuring**

As you build your structure, AI offers:

- **Real-Time Gap Detection:** "You have a strong setup but no clear midpoint. Consider what changes at the story's center."
- **Thread Balance Alerts:** "Your antagonist appears in only 3 scenes. For a story of this length, consider 8-12 appearances for effective antagonist development."
- **Arc Progress Tracking:** "Character A's arc has no visible low point. Most redemption arcs include a moment of greatest failure before transformation."

**Step 3: Pre-Draft Review**

Before moving to Manuscript, AI provides:

- **Structural Health Dashboard:** Comprehensive analysis of pacing, thread balance, character arcs, and structural completeness
- **Priority Recommendations:** Ranked list of structural improvements, from critical to optional
- **Readiness Score:** Assessment of whether structure is ready for drafting or needs more development

**Step 4: Drafting Support**

While drafting, AI offers:

- **Scene Context:** When opening a scene, show its position in structure, connected threads, and upcoming beats
- **Mid-Draft Check-ins:** After completing scenes, AI may suggest: "This scene advances the romance thread but delays the mystery reveal. Consider adding a clue here to maintain thread balance."
- **Progress Tracking:** Visual indicators of structural completion alongside word count

**Step 5: Revision Intelligence**

After drafting, AI provides:

- **Structural Fidelity Analysis:** How well the final draft matches intended structure
- **Discovery Documentation:** Highlights unexpected structural elements that emerged during drafting
- **Revision Prioritization:** Identifies which structural issues most impact reader experience

## **7. The Binder (Outliner)**

### **Overview**

The Binder is the hierarchical backbone of every Canvas project. Inspired by Scrivener's renowned binder, it provides a clear, navigable structure for organizing all elements of a writing project—from folders and chapters to individual scenes and research notes.

**Core Concept:** Everything has a place, and every place is easy to find.

### **Features**

### **Hierarchical Architecture**

- **Flexible Nesting:** Folders → Parts → Chapters → Scenes → Sub-scenes (unlimited depth)
- **Mixed Content Types:** Manuscript documents, research notes, character profiles, location entries, images—all in one binder
- **Visual Differentiation:** Custom icons and color coding for different content types (manuscript, research, character, etc.)
- **Collapse/Expand:** Collapse sections for high-level overview; expand for granular detail
- **Quick Search:** Spotlight-style search to jump to any item instantly
- **Multi-Project Support:** A "Universe Vault" allows for series-level planning, sharing characters and lore across multiple books

### **Drag-and-Drop Architecture**

- **Reordering:** Drag any item to reorder within its level
- **Restructuring:** Move chapters between parts, scenes between chapters, entire sections anywhere
- **Smart Updates:** All internal links, references, and cross-references update automatically
- **Batch Operations:** Select multiple items to move, delete, or change status together
- **Undo/Redo:** Full support for accidental moves with history

### **Rich Metadata System**

| **Metadata Type**  | **Description**                                        | **Use Case**                                                |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Status**         | Draft stage (Outline, First Draft, Revision, Complete) | Track progress through writing stages                       |
| **Priority**       | Critical, Important, Optional, Stretch                 | Identify essential scenes vs. expandable content            |
| **POV**            | Which character narrates                               | Track POV distribution across manuscript                    |
| **Timeline Date**  | When scene occurs                                      | Maintain chronological consistency                          |
| **Location**       | Where scene takes place                                | Track location usage, avoid repetition                      |
| **Emotional Tone** | Mood of the scene                                      | Ensure emotional variety and arc                            |
| **Plot Threads**   | Which threads appear                                   | Track subplot appearances                                   |
| **Custom Fields**  | User-defined fields                                    | Any tracking needs (e.g., "Contains Magic," "Romance Beat") |

### **The Architect's Desk (Outliner View)**

- **Table Layout:** Switch from hierarchical tree to customizable table view
- **Visible Columns:** Display any combination of metadata fields
- **Inline Editing:** Edit metadata directly in table without opening documents
- **Sorting:** Sort by any column to identify patterns (e.g., sort by POV to see distribution)
- **Filtering:** Show only scenes matching criteria (e.g., "POV = Amina AND Status = First Draft")
- **Calculations:** Auto-summary of word counts, scene counts per chapter/act
- **Bulk Editing:** Quickly update statuses or labels for multiple scenes simultaneously
- **Export:** Export outline as CSV, Markdown, or directly to project templates

### **AI-Powered Binder Intelligence**

- **Structure Health Indicators:** Visual icons showing which sections have structural issues
- **Completion Forecasting:** AI predicts when you'll complete each section based on writing patterns
- **Scene Necessity Analysis:** Flags scenes that may be redundant or underdeveloped
- **Auto-Organization Suggestions:** AI suggests logical grouping of related scenes (user approval required)

### **Integration with Sandbox & Idea Web**

- **Pull from Sandbox:** Drag developed characters, settings, or plot points into binder structure
- **Promote to Scene:** Convert any Sandbox element into a binder scene with one click
- **Linked References:** Binder items maintain links to source Sandbox entries
- **Auto-Populate:** Create scene structure from selected Sandbox elements

### **Use Case: The Epic Architect**

**Scenario:** Kwame is writing a five-book epic fantasy with multiple POVs, complex worldbuilding, and interweaving plotlines.

**Binder Workflow:**

1. Creates top-level structure: "Book 1: The Awakening" → Parts 1-3 → Chapters → Scenes
2. Assigns metadata: POV character, timeline date, location for every scene
3. Uses Architect's Desk table view to ensure each POV appears regularly across chapters
4. Sets status flags to track first draft completion
5. Filters to show only scenes with "POV = Antagonist" to check character development
6. Exports timeline table to share with beta readers

**Benefits:** Complete architectural control. Complex structures remain navigable. Progress is measurable.

## **8. The Corkboard**

### **Overview**

The Corkboard transforms your story into a visual landscape of index cards. Each card represents a scene, displaying key information at a glance. Writers can arrange, reorder, and cluster cards to see narrative flow, identify pacing issues, and experiment with structure. This visual tool mimics a physical corkboard with index cards, allowing users to visualize the novel's structure and shuffle cards to fix pacing issues.

**Core Concept:** See the whole story at a glance. Move pieces until they fit.

### **Features**

### **Dynamic Index Cards**

## **17. Technical Specifications**

### **Stack Alignment**

- Built on OdinPad stack: React/Next.js + Supabase (PostgreSQL, Auth, Storage, Realtime)
- Canvas-based rendering for Corkboard, Timeline, and Atlas using React Flow + Konva
- WebAssembly for client-side analysis (pacing, thread balance)
- Real-time sync for Observatory views
- Offline-first via PWA + IndexedDB caching
- Virtualized rendering and lazy loading for large projects (500+ cards, 1,000+ nodes)

| ---------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Project**      | id, user_id, title, structure_type, created_at, settings                                       |

| **Scene**        | id, binder_item_id, synopsis, status, pov, location, timeline_date, emotional_tone, thread_ids |
| **Card**         | id, scene_id, position_x, position_y, card_color, expanded_view                                |
| **Timeline**     | id, project_id, name, settings                                                                 |

| **TimelineCard** | id, timeline_id, row_id, scene_id, column_start, column_end                                    |
