**Sandbox: The Creative Expansion Engine**

---

**1. Vision & Purpose**

The **Sandbox** is OdinPad's dedicated creative expansion engine. Writers can converse with their story elements, build massive lists of possibilities, visually map relationships, and organically grow fragments into structured content. It serves as the crucial bridge between "I have an idea" and "I have a plan."

Unlike rigid outlining tools, Sandbox remains flexible and forgiving: freeform notes, visual diagrams, prompt sparks, and AI-assisted organization help overcome blocks while keeping everything connected to the rest of OdinPad. It encourages playful exploration ("What if?" rabbit holes, random combinations) and rewards consistent engagement via OdinPad’s **Gamification System**.

Aligned with OdinPad's **AI Philosophy (0% generative text)** , the Sandbox AI acts as a **"Creative Sparring Partner"** —it asks the right questions, spots inconsistencies, and suggests directions, but never writes the prose for the user.

**Core Philosophy:** Where the Idea Web ensures no idea is lost, the Sandbox ensures every idea has room to grow. Together, they form the creative engine of OdinPad: capture, expand, develop, draft.

**For the writer, this means:** Less time staring at blank pages. More time exploring possibilities. A clear path from "what if" to "what's next." And the confidence that when you sit down to draft, your ideas are ready to become stories.

---

**2. Core Philosophy: Expansion Over Capture**

The Sandbox operates on a different principle than the Idea Web:

|                 |                    |                                       |
| --------------- | ------------------ | ------------------------------------- |
| **Principle**   | **Idea Web**       | **Sandbox**                           |
| **Focus**       | Capture & preserve | Expand & develop                      |
| **Input**       | Quick fragments    | Deliberate exploration                |
| **Output**      | Seeds & snippets   | Developed elements ready for drafting |
| **Mindset**     | "Don't lose this"  | "What else could this be?"            |
| **Interaction** | Passive collection | Active creation                       |

While the Idea Web is about *capturing what you have*, the Sandbox is about *discovering what you don't have yet*—exploring possibilities, generating alternatives, and building depth before committing to a draft.

**Core Philosophy Fit:**

- Defeats writer's block by providing gentle, opt-in prompts and tools
- Prevents lost momentum: Captures "brain dumps" instantly, organizes them organically
- Bridges Idea Web → Canvas/Manuscript: Promoted brainstorm items flow seamlessly into Story Canvas cards, Story Wiki entries, or draft snippets
- AI strictly assistant: Detection (e.g., entity recognition), suggestions (e.g., "Link this to existing character?"), visualizations—no text generation

**3. The Sandbox Components**

The Sandbox consists of six interconnected tools, each designed for a different mode of creative expansion:

1. **Conversational Brainstorm** — Interactive dialogue with story elements
2. **The Braindump** — Freeform capture and organization
3. **List Builder** — Massive idea generation and iteration
4. **Visual Sandbox** — Mind maps, trees, and spatial organization
5. **Prompt Engine** — Intelligent inspiration generation
6. **Expansion Canvas** — Deep development of individual elements

---

**4. Conversational Brainstorm**

**Overview**

The Conversational Brainstorm tool allows writers to develop ideas through direct, interactive dialogue with their story elements. Unlike traditional Sandbox (which is often solitary and linear), this tool creates a dynamic conversation where writers can question, challenge, and explore with their characters, settings, and plot elements as active participants.

**Core Concept:** You can't just think *about* your characters—you can think *with* them.

**Features**

**Persona Chat (Character Conversations)**

- **Chat with Your Characters:** Select any character from your Story Wiki and enter a conversational interface. Ask questions, explore motivations, test dialogue, or simply let them talk.
- **Voice Consistency:** AI maintains character voice based on your established profile (speech patterns, vocabulary, personality traits, background).
- **Question Prompts:** Pre-built question templates for character development:
  - "What do you want more than anything?"
  - "What are you afraid of?"
  - "What would you never do, even to save yourself?"
  - "Tell me about your childhood."
  - "What do you think of [other character]?"
- **Socratic Exploration:** Ask "why" repeatedly to dig into deeper motivations. The conversation evolves as you probe.

**Element Stress-Test**

- **Talk to Your Setting:** "What secrets are hidden in the old forest?" "What does this city smell like at dawn?" "Who holds power here that no one talks about?"
- **Talk to Your Magic System:** "What are the costs I haven't considered?" "How would someone break these rules?" "What does this magic *feel* like to use?"
- **Talk to Your Faction:** "What do you want that you don't have?" "Who is your greatest enemy?" "What would make you ally with your enemy?"
- **Talk to Your Theme:** "What am I really trying to say here?" "How does this theme manifest in unexpected ways?" "What's the opposite of my theme, and where does it appear?"

**Plot & Scenario Exploration**

- **What-If Conversations:** Describe a scenario and "discuss" it with your plot elements. Example: "What if the heist goes wrong at the very first step?"
- **Conflict Testing:** Introduce obstacles to characters and see how they respond. "A guard spots you. What do you do?"
- **Branching Exploration:** Explore multiple paths for a single decision point, saving promising branches for later development.
- **The "What-If" Sparring:** A dedicated chat mode where the AI suggests alternative directions:

_User: "What if the protagonist refuses the call to adventure?"_

_AI: "That would shift the stakes. Would the antagonist then target their family to force their hand, or would a secondary character have to step up, changing the story's theme to 'unlikely heroes'?"_

**Integration with Idea Web**

- Pull any Idea Web entry into a conversation as the subject
- Save conversation threads as new ideas or expanded notes
- Export promising dialogue as character voice samples or scene fragments

**Use Case: The Stuck Writer**

**Scenario:** Adjoa knows her protagonist is supposed to betray someone, but she doesn't understand why he would do it.

**Conversational Brainstorm:**

1. She opens Conversational Brainstorm with protagonist Kofi
2. Asks: "Kofi, tell me about the person you're closest to."
3. Kofi (in his voice): "My sister, Amma. She raised me after our parents died. I'd do anything for her."
4. Adjoa asks: "What if someone threatened Amma?"
5. Kofi: "I'd burn the world down to keep her safe."
6. Adjoa realizes: the betrayal isn't about greed—it's about protecting his sister.

**Result:** A character motivation that unlocks the entire third act, discovered through conversation rather than struggle.

**5. The Braindump**

**Overview**

The Braindump is a friction-free zone for capturing freeform notes, research snippets, half-formed thoughts, and anything that doesn't yet have a home. Unlike the Idea Web (which focuses on discrete, taggable ideas), the Braindump is designed for flow—stream-of-consciousness entry that can later be parsed, organized, and developed.

**Core Concept:** Get it *out* before you figure out *where it goes*.

**Features**

**Freeform Capture**

- **Infinite Canvas:** A scrollable, unbounded text area—no structure required, no formatting demands. Supports Markdown, rich text, bullet lists, numbered steps, tables, or pure chaos.
- **Voice-to-Text:** Mobile-optimized voice capture for dictating thoughts while walking, driving, or resting. Auto-transcribed and saved with timestamp.
- **Timestamped Entries:** Each session creates a dated container; individual entries within sessions are timestamped for context.
- **Tag-as-You-Go:** Optional inline tagging with #character #plot #research etc.—tags become searchable without imposing structure.
- **Auto-Save:** Every few seconds; nothing lost.

**Research Integration**

- **Snippet Import:** Drag research articles, PDFs, or web clippings directly into the Braindump. Key sections automatically extracted.
- **Source Tracking:** Automatically preserve source URLs, publication dates, and author information.
- **Highlight Extraction:** Pull quotes and highlights from imported research, preserving context and source attribution.
- **Multi-Modal Input:** Support for text, voice-to-text (transcribed), and image uploads.

**Idea Web Integration**

- **Pull from Idea Web:** Select Idea Web entries to expand upon in the Braindump.
- **Push to Idea Web:** Convert any Braindump section into a structured idea with one click.
- **Two-Way Sync:** Changes in either direction can optionally reflect across both tools.

**Organization Tools**

- **Fold & Expand:** Collapse sections to manage long braindumps; expand as needed.
- **Color Coding:** Highlight sections by theme, importance, or emotional tone.
- **Search & Filter:** Full-text search across all braindumps; filter by tags, date ranges, or source types.
- **Export Sections:** Select specific sections to export as notes, ideas, or research entries.
- **Smart Lists:** A "massive list builder" for names, dialogue snippets, sensory details, and world-building "flavor" within the braindump context.

**Use Case: The Research-Heavy Writer**

**Scenario:** Kwame is researching 19th-century Gold Coast history for a historical novel. He has PDFs, website clippings, interview notes, and his own scattered thoughts.

**Braindump Workflow:**

1. Drags PDFs into Braindump—key sections automatically extracted
2. Adds voice notes after interviews: "Elder said the Asantehene's court had a 'whisperer'—someone who knew everyone's secrets. Need to fact-check."
3. Tags sections with #research #asante #court-intrigue
4. As connections emerge, he highlights sections and pushes them to Idea Web as character seeds
5. When ready to outline, he exports all #asante tagged sections to a project folder

**Result:** Research never gets lost. Insights captured in the moment become the foundation for authentic worldbuilding.

**6. List Builder**

**Overview**

The List Builder is a massive idea generation engine designed to help writers create extensive lists of possibilities—character names, plot twists, magical artifacts, dialogue snippets, setting details, and everything in between. Rather than struggling to come up with *the* perfect idea, writers can generate dozens or hundreds of options and discover the best ones through comparison and combination.

**Core Concept:** Creativity is a numbers game. Generate more, choose better.

**Features**

**List Types**

|                           |                                                   |                                                                                 |
| ------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **List Type**             | **Purpose**                                       | **Examples**                                                                    |
| **Character Names**       | Generate names by culture, meaning, sound, or era | West African fantasy names, sci-fi ship names, Victorian surnames, dragon names |
| **Plot Points**           | Story beats, twists, obstacles, revelations       | "A betrayal from an unexpected ally," "The solution creates a worse problem"    |
| **Worldbuilding Details** | Locations, customs, technologies, flora/fauna     | "A market that only appears at full moon," "Currency that decays over time"     |
| **Dialogue Lines**        | Snippets, quotes, comebacks, opening lines        | "The dead don't stay dead around here." "You're asking the wrong question."     |
| **Magic/Technology**      | Systems, devices, costs, limitations              | "A compass that points to what you've lost," "Memory-powered engines"           |
| **Themes & Motifs**       | Recurring ideas, symbols, philosophical questions | "Identity vs. belonging," "The cost of progress"                                |
| **Conflicts**             | Internal/external struggles, dilemmas             | "Choose between saving one or many," "Loyalty to family vs. justice"            |
| **Character Traits**      | Personality, flaws, quirks, backgrounds           | "Can't lie—physically incapable," "Collects useless trivia"                     |
| **Sensory Details**       | Sights, sounds, smells, textures, tastes          | "Rain on hot pavement," "Smell of ozone before magic"                           |

**Generation Methods**

- **AI-Assisted Expansion:** Start with one idea, AI suggests 10+ variations or alternatives
- **Combination Engine:** Combine two list entries to generate hybrids (e.g., name + trait = "A healer who causes pain")
- **Random Generation:** Pull random items from your existing lists for inspiration
- **Template-Based:** Use structured templates (e.g., Save the Cat beat sheet, Hero's Journey stages) to generate plot points
- **Constraint-Based:** Generate within creative constraints ("Only names starting with K," "Conflicts that can't be solved with violence")
- **Randomizer Tools:** Pull existing elements from Idea Web/Story Wiki → combine randomly (e.g., Character + Setting + Conflict = prompt "A forgetful wizard in a memory-eating forest faces betrayal")

**List Management**

- **Collections:** Group lists by project, genre, or theme
- **Favorites:** Mark promising entries for further development
- **Ranking:** Rate entries to surface best options
- **Cross-Referencing:** Link list entries to Story Wiki elements
- **Export:** Export lists as CSV, Markdown, or directly to project templates

**Integration with Other Tools**

- Push promising list entries to Idea Web for cultivation
- Expand list entries into full elements via Expansion Canvas
- Use list entries as prompts in Conversational Brainstorm

**Use Case: The Worldbuilder**

**Scenario:** Efua is building a fantasy city with 12 distinct districts. She needs each district to have a unique identity, economy, and conflict.

**List Builder Workflow:**

1. Creates "Districts" list—generates 20 district names using cultural naming patterns
2. Selects 12 favorites, pushes each to a "District Development" list
3. For each district, generates:

- 5-10 economic activities
- 5-8 conflicts unique to that district
- 3-5 secrets or hidden elements

4. Uses Combination Engine to merge "Fisher's Wharf" + "Forbidden Magic" = a district where fishermen secretly use forbidden magic to guarantee catches
5. Pushes developed districts to Story Wiki

**Result:** A richly developed city with depth and conflict in every district, built systematically rather than exhausting the writer's mental energy.

**7. Visual Sandbox**

**Overview**

Visual Sandbox provides spatial, non-linear tools for exploring relationships between ideas. For writers who think visually, or for complex projects where linear outlines feel limiting, these tools offer alternative ways to see connections, hierarchies, and flows.

**Core Concept:** Sometimes you need to *see* it to *understand* it.

All views support infinite canvas (no artificial boundaries), zoom/pan, color themes (cozy defaults like parchment/ink, unlockable via Gamification), undo/redo, and real-time autosave.

**Features**

**Mind Maps & Organic Branching (Core & Most Flexible)**

- **Central Node:** Start with a core idea, character, or theme (or import an Idea Web seed)
- **Freeform Placement:** Like Scapple, drop notes anywhere on the canvas—no forced hierarchy at first; draw connections manually or auto-suggest via AI
- **Branching:** Add child nodes for related concepts, expanding outward
- **Color Coding:** By category, importance, emotional tone, or custom groups
- **Collapsible Branches:** Focus on specific areas while maintaining context
- **Rich Nodes:** Each node supports mini-rich text (Markdown toggle), attachments (images, mood board pins, links to Story Wiki entries), status tags (e.g., "Promising," "Needs Work"), and collapse/expand
- **Export:** PNG/SVG image, interactive HTML, or direct promote branches as Story Canvas cards or Story Wiki stubs
- **Benefits:** Ideal for initial "big picture" exploration; pantsers love the organic feel, plotters use it to prototype three-act or Hero's Journey beats

**Trees & Hierarchical Diagrams (Structured Refinement)**

- **Tree View Mode:** Converts mind map to clean top-down tree (or sideways for horizontal flow)
- **Nested Levels:** Root → Major arcs → Chapters/acts → Scenes/beats → Micro-details
- **Drag-Reorder Nodes:** Indent/outdent for hierarchy; auto-collapse distant branches
- **Templates:** Pre-built skeletons (Save the Cat beats, Snowflake method layers, 3-Act structure, Hero's Journey) that users populate or customize
- **AI Assistance:** Suggests logical child nodes based on detected patterns—no new content generated
- **Specific Trees:** Family trees (character relationships), organizational trees (factions, governments), plot trees (branching paths), thematic trees, timeline trees
- **Benefits:** Helps transition from chaos to outline; great for series authors mapping multi-book arcs

**Affinity Diagrams & Clustering (Pattern Discovery)**

- **Sticky-Note Canvas:** Drag in Idea Web cards, Story Wiki excerpts, or new notes → group visually (draw boxes, color clusters)
- **Auto-Cluster Detection:** AI scans for thematic resonance (shared tags, keywords, moods) → suggests groups (e.g., "All 'betrayal' ideas cluster here—potential motif?"). User approves/moves.
- **Connection Lines:** Draw manual arrows (cause-effect, contrast, parallel) or let AI propose
- **Benefits:** Surfaces recurring themes or contradictions early; excellent for worldbuilding (group cultures, magic rules) or character development (affinity map flaws vs. strengths)

**Spatial Organization & Flowcharts**

- **Freeform Canvas:** Place ideas anywhere; draw connections between them
- **Proximity Grouping:** Ideas placed near each other suggest relationships
- **Zone Creation:** Define regions of the canvas for different categories (characters here, plot there, setting there)
- **Layer Management:** Multiple layers for different aspects of the same project
- **Flowcharts / Process Maps:** For plotting cause-effect chains (e.g., "If hero chooses door A → consequence B")
- **Decision Trees:** Visualize character choices and their consequences
- **Scene Sequencing:** Arrange and rearrange scene order visually
- **Arc Tracking:** Map character development across the narrative

**Radial & Concept Maps (Thematic/Relational Exploration)**

- **Radial Layout:** Central idea surrounded by orbiting concepts (e.g., "World Magic System" → orbiting nodes for rules, costs, examples, limitations, cultural impact)
- **Concept Map Mode:** More relational than strict hierarchy—use labeled arrows (e.g., "causes," "contradicts," "enhances") between any nodes
- **Zoomable Overview:** Collapse to high-level bubbles; expand for details
- **Benefits:** Perfect for thematic Sandbox ("How does 'sacrifice' echo across plot/characters/setting?") or exploring interconnected lore

**Additional Diagram Types & Enhancements**

- **Venn Diagrams / Overlap Views:** Compare elements (e.g., overlapping character motivations vs. antagonist goals)
- **Custom Layouts:** Switch between auto-arranged (force-directed for organic spread) and manual drag
- **Layering & Filters:** Toggle visibility (e.g., show only "Character" nodes, hide "Worldbuilding"); multiple layers for "Draft 1 Ideas" vs. "Revised"
- **Performance:** Virtualized rendering (handles 500+ nodes smoothly); lazy-load distant areas

**AI Editorial Support (Non-Generative)**

- **Pattern Detection:** Highlight potential clusters, orphans (unconnected ideas), or conflicts (e.g., "This branch contradicts Story Wiki magic rule")
- **Suggestions:** "Link this node to existing Wiki entry 'Elara'?" or "This cluster has 8+ betrayal motifs—strong theme candidate?"
- **Plot Hole Alerts:** AI identifies missing links (e.g., *"Character X is in London in Chapter 2 but appears in Tokyo in Chapter 3 without a travel scene. How did they get there?"*)
- **User Control:** Approve, dismiss, or mark "ignore forever"; intelligence improves via feedback loop

**Integration with Idea Web**

- Import Idea Web entries as map nodes
- Visual connections can become Idea Web connections
- Export map clusters as new Idea Web collections

**Use Case: The Complex Plotter**

**Scenario:** Kofi's mystery novel has 14 suspects, 3 detectives, and a timeline spanning 20 years. His outline is a tangled mess.

**Visual Sandbox Workflow:**

1. Creates a timeline tree showing all events across the 20 years
2. Builds a family tree of all suspects and their relationships to the victim
3. Creates a flowchart of the investigation—each clue leading to new suspects
4. Uses color coding: red for false leads, green for real clues, yellow for character motives
5. Spots a gap: a suspect has no connection to the timeline—discovers a plot hole before writing
6. Exports the cleaned structure to Story Canvas for drafting

**Result:** A complex plot becomes visually comprehensible. Plot holes discovered in planning, not revision.

**8. Prompt Engine**

**Overview**

The Prompt Engine generates intelligent, context-aware prompts designed to spark ideas, overcome blocks, and push thinking in new directions. Unlike generic prompt generators, the Prompt Engine integrates with your existing ideas, projects, and creative history to generate prompts that are relevant to *your* work.

**Core Concept:** The best prompts are the ones that matter to *your* story.

**Features**

**Prompt Types**

|                         |                                 |                                                                                                                                                                                |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Prompt Type**         | **Purpose**                     | **Example**                                                                                                                                                                    |
| **Expansion Prompts**   | Develop existing ideas deeper   | "Your character wants something. What's the one thing they'd _never_ do to get it?"                                                                                            |
| **Connection Prompts**  | Find links between ideas        | "How does your magic system reflect your protagonist's internal conflict?"                                                                                                     |
| **Obstacle Prompts**    | Introduce meaningful challenges | "What if the solution to your plot's central problem creates an even worse problem?"                                                                                           |
| **Perspective Prompts** | Shift viewpoint                 | "Tell this scene from the antagonist's perspective. What are they thinking?"                                                                                                   |
| **Constraint Prompts**  | Work within limits              | "Write a version of this scene without dialogue."                                                                                                                              |
| **What-If Prompts**     | Explore alternatives            | "What if the mentor figure died in Chapter 2 instead of Chapter 15?"                                                                                                           |
| **Research Prompts**    | Identify gaps                   | "What would an expert in 18th-century navigation notice about your ship's description?"                                                                                        |
| **Theme Prompts**       | Deepen meaning                  | "What does your story say about forgiveness? Where does it say it most clearly?"                                                                                               |
| **The Unblocker**       | Break through stalls            | If a user hasn't written in a specific scene for days: _"Write a 200-word scene of [Character A] and [Character B] arguing about a mundane chore to find their unique voice."_ |
| **Wiki-Driven Prompts** | Fill gaps                       | _"You haven't defined the 'limitations' of your magic system yet. What happens if a user over-exerts themselves?"_                                                             |

**Intelligent Integration**

- **Project-Aware:** Prompts reference your actual characters, settings, and plot points
- **History-Aware:** Avoids repeating prompts you've already explored
- **Progress-Aware:** Different prompts for early Sandbox vs. revision phases
- **Genre-Aware:** Genre-appropriate prompts (mystery prompts differ from romance prompts)

**Idea Web Integration**

- **Idea-Specific Prompts:** Pull any Idea Web entry and generate prompts specifically for expanding it
- **Seed Combinator:** Takes two random "Sprouts" from the Idea Web and asks the user to find a connection
- **Evolution Prompts:** Asks the user to take a "Dormant" idea and "Sprout" it by adding a specific conflict or character
- **Prompt-to-Idea:** Save generated prompts as new ideas or attach them to existing ones
- **Prompt History:** Track which prompts you've used and what they generated

**Daily Prompt & Collections**

- **Seed of the Day:** A fresh prompt delivered to your dashboard daily
- **Streak Integration:** Responding to prompts counts toward Writer's Odyssey streaks
- **Prompt Collections:** Themed prompt sets (e.g., "Character Development Week," "Plot Twist Month")
- **User Controls:** Filter by type (character, plot, world, theme), tone (dark, whimsical), or length

**Use Case: The Blocked Writer**

**Scenario:** Ama has been staring at the same scene for three days. She knows what happens, but it feels flat.

**Prompt Engine Workflow:**

1. Opens Prompt Engine, selects "Obstacle Prompts" for the current scene
2. Prompt generates: "What's the worst thing that could happen to your protagonist right now that you haven't considered?"
3. She realizes: the scene is flat because there's no tension. The worst thing? Her character's hidden secret is exposed—not to the villain, but to the ally she trusts most.
4. Rewrites the scene with this new complication
5. Saves the prompt and her response for future reference

**Result:** A blocked scene becomes the turning point of the chapter. She uses the Prompt Engine regularly to push past comfortable choices.

**9. Expansion Canvas**

**Overview**

The Expansion Canvas is a dedicated environment for taking a single idea—a character, a location, a plot point, a magic system—and developing it into a fully fleshed-out element ready for the Story Wiki or Story Canvas.

**Core Concept:** Deep work on one thing at a time.

**Features**

**Structured Expansion Templates**

|                  |                                                                                                            |
| ---------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Element Type** | **Expansion Sections**                                                                                     |
| **Character**    | Core traits, backstory, motivation, flaws, arc, voice, relationships, secrets, physical description, goals |
| **Location**     | Physical description, history, culture, inhabitants, conflicts, secrets, atmosphere, economy               |
| **Plot Point**   | Setup, execution, consequences, alternatives, foreshadowing, emotional impact                              |
| **Magic/System** | Rules, costs, limitations, applications, history, exceptions, cultural impact                              |
| **Theme**        | Manifestations, conflicts, character relationships, symbols, resolution, counter-themes                    |
| **Faction**      | Structure, goals, leadership, resources, enemies, allies, secrets                                          |
| **Scene**        | Setting, characters, conflict, stakes, outcome, emotional beat, setup/payoff                               |

**Expansion Methods**

- **Question-Based:** Pre-built question sets that guide deep exploration (Socratic method)
- **Spider-Web:** Start with central concept, add connected elements in all directions
- **Contrast:** Define by what it is and what it isn't
- **Evolution:** Track how this element changes across the narrative
- **Integration:** How does this element connect to everything else?

**Relationship Mapping**

- Visual map of how this element connects to others in your Story Wiki
- Identify gaps: "This character has no relationship with the antagonist—should they?"
- Suggest new connections based on existing elements

**Idea Web Integration**

- Pull any Idea Web entry into Expansion Canvas
- Save expansion results as enhanced ideas or push to Story Wiki
- Track expansion progress with status indicators

**Use Case: The Character Developer**

**Scenario:** Yaw has a character concept: "A former soldier who now runs a tea shop." He knows this is the protagonist, but he needs depth.

**Expansion Canvas Workflow:**

1. Pulls the character seed from Idea Web into Expansion Canvas
2. Works through Character template:

- **Backstory:** "Why did she leave the army? What was the last straw?"
- **Motivation:** "Why tea? What does the shop represent to her?"
- **Flaws:** "She still thinks like a soldier—plans everything, trusts no one"
- **Voice:** "Formal speech patterns, military precision even when discussing tea"
- **Arc:** "Learning that not everything is a battle. Some things are gardens."

3. Relationship map shows she has no connections—builds a mentor figure who was her commanding officer
4. When satisfied, pushes to Story Wiki with all development preserved

**Result:** A shallow concept becomes a fully realized character with arc, voice, and relationships—ready for drafting.

**10. The Sandbox Workflow: From Idea to Draft**

The Sandbox is designed as a coherent workflow, not isolated tools. Here's how they connect:

text

Idea Web (Capture)

    ↓

Braindump (Freeform Expansion)

    ↓

List Builder (Generate Options)

    ↓

Visual Sandbox (See Connections)

    ↓

Expansion Canvas (Deep Development)

    ↓

Prompt Engine (Push Further)

    ↓

Conversational Brainstorm (Test & Refine)

    ↓

Story Wiki / Story Canvas (Ready to Draft)

But writers can enter at any point and move between tools organically:

- Start with a conversation, save insights to Braindump
- Generate lists, visualize connections, expand promising elements
- Use prompts when stuck, return to conversation to test developed ideas
- Harvest any successful brainstorm directly into actionable story material

|                  |                                                     |
| ---------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| **Destination**  | **What Happens**                                    |

- Drag items/lists/branches to destination
- Keyboard shortcut for quick promotion
- Bulk select multiple brainstorm items → promote together

**Two-Way Sync**

- Changes in promoted content optionally reflect back to brainstorm source
- Example: Character name updated in Story Wiki → prompts to update related brainstorm references
- Configurable per item: "sync updates" or "freeze original"
- Useful for tracking how an idea evolved during drafting

**Workspace Placement**

- Dedicated Brainstorm tab/mode in OdinPad Workspace
- Split-view with Idea Web, Story Wiki, or Manuscript
- Quick capture from within any context
